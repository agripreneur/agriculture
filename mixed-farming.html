Mixed farming is a system where crops and livestock are raised together on the same farm. It helps farmers efficiently use resources, increase income, and maintain sustainability through mutual support between plant and animal production.

üîÅ Key Features of Mixed Farming

  • Dual Activity: Farming includes both crops and animals.
  • Resource Sharing: Manure for soil, crop waste for feed.
  • Risk Diversification: Reduces dependency on one type of farming.
  • Improved Soil Fertility: Naturally fertilized with livestock waste.
  • Better Income Stability: Two income sources from the same farm.

üêÑ Common Components in Mixed Farming

  • Crops: Wheat, maize, vegetables, rice.

  • By-products: Manure, compost, animal feed from crop residues.
