Traditional farming refers to ancient agricultural practices based on indigenous knowledge, natural inputs, and minimal use of machinery or synthetic chemicals. It is sustainable, low-cost, and closely connected to local ecosystems and cultures.

  • Low Capital: Requires little investment in equipment or chemicals.

  • Nomadic Herding: Moving animals for grazing based on seasons.
  • Mixed Farming: Raising both crops and animals traditionally.
