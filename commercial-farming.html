Commercial farming is the large-scale production of crops and livestock primarily for sale in markets. It is a profit-driven agricultural system that uses modern techniques and technology to produce food and raw materials on a commercial scale.

üîë Key Features of Commercial Farming

  • Market-Oriented: Focused entirely on selling produce for profit.
  • Large-Scale: Farms typically span vast areas.
  • Mechanized: Uses tractors, harvesters, and irrigation systems.
  • Capital Intensive: High investments in inputs and infrastructure.
  • Specialized: Often focuses on one or two products.

üåæ Types of Commercial Farming

  • Commercial Grain Farming: Wheat, barley, and corn.
  • Commercial Dairy Farming: Milk and dairy products.
  • Plantation Farming: Tea, coffee, rubber, sugarcane.
  • Livestock Ranching: Cattle, sheep, goats on large grazing lands.
